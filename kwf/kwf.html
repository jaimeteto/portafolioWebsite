Internship Experience

Kashmir World Foundation

Software Engineering Intern / Team Lead

June 2025 – September 2025

Visit Organization

Overview

Kashmir World Foundation is a nonprofit organization focused on conservation through technology and research. During my internship, I contributed to the development of software tools used by researchers to collect and analyze environmental data in the field. My work focused on building features across both mobile and desktop platforms, improving data reliability, and designing a syncing system that works without constant internet access.

Tech Stack

React Native, Expo, TypeScript, SQLite (Drizzle ORM), Electron, Node.js, REST APIs, GitHub, Figma

My Role

  • Worked on mobile and desktop applications used for research data collection
  • Helped design and implement syncing workflows between mobile and desktop systems
  • Contributed to building offline-first features for use in remote environments
  • Collaborated with team members on architecture and feature decisions
  • Took on leadership responsibilities, helping guide parts of development

Challenges & Problem Solving

One of the main challenges was ensuring reliable data syncing between mobile and desktop applications, especially in offline scenarios. I worked on improving how data was structured and transferred, helping ensure consistency and reliability across systems. This required thinking carefully about edge cases like conflicts, timestamps, and missing data.

What I Learned

This experience helped me grow both technically and as a leader. I learned how to design systems that work in real-world conditions, think through data consistency challenges, and collaborate effectively on a complex project. It also gave me experience working on software that directly supports research and environmental impact.
